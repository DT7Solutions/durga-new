The foundation of Durga Ghee was laid when our founder, driven by a passion for purity and authenticity, began sourcing fresh butter directly from farmers and processing it into traditional ghee. The journey started with a humble door-to-door sales approach, delivering high-quality ghee packed in eco-friendly materials.

Durga Ghee transitioned from an unorganized setup to a structured, quality-driven brand. The commitment to maintaining traditional ghee-making methods while ensuring consistency led to increased customer trust.

Durga Ghee became a recognized brand in the dairy industry. With a focus on purity and premium quality, the company introduced hygienic packaging and modernized its distribution, making pure cow ghee accessible across regions.

Durga Ghee received Agmark certification, reinforcing its commitment to purity and quality assurance.

The establishment of a modern manufacturing unit marked a new era. Using advanced technology while preserving traditional slow-cooking methods, Durga Ghee set industry benchmarks for purity and taste.

A significant milestone was achieved with the launch of Durga Ghee’s first-ever television commercial, increasing brand awareness and reaching a wider audience.

Durga Ghee expanded its presence internationally, making its way into markets in Singapore and Malaysia.

Durga Ghee diversified its product range, venturing into dairy-based offerings and premium quality ice creams.

A dedicated production unit was set up for manufacturing premium masalas and instant mixes, expanding Durga Ghee’s portfolio in the culinary segment.

Durga Ghee achieved a significant business milestone, crossing ₹100 crore in turnover.

Recognizing the demand for traditional sweets, Durga Ghee introduced a range of ready-to-eat Indian sweets, backed by a dedicated production facility.

Durga Ghee expanded into the snacks segment under the brand name 'Town Bus,' launching a state-of-the-art manufacturing unit.

In 1987, the foundation for the future Durga Ghee brand was laid with small-scale buffalo ghee production. Sourcing fresh milk locally, we began hand-churning ghee in traditional methods — ensuring purity and richness in every batch.

By 1989, we expanded to door-to-door deliveries and began packaging ghee in eco-friendly tins, building trust among households and sweet makers in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.

In 1991, Durgadairy was officially established — bringing structured operations for milk and ghee production, and setting the stage for branded packaging and quality control in our buffalo ghee range.

We introduced modern machinery to process buffalo ghee in larger volumes without compromising on traditional simmering techniques, paving the way for scale and consistency.

Durga Ghee began retailing in local stores, and gained recognition for its unmatched taste and aroma — becoming a staple in homes and temples alike.

We introduced tamper-proof packaging and multiple size options in 1999, enhancing shelf life and expanding reach into new towns and rural markets.

By 2001, Durga Ghee was a household name in Southern India. We began investing in branding and outreach with flyers, vans, and product demos at fairs.

In 2005, the Durga Ghee brand was officially launched, focusing on both buffalo and cow ghee — made using slow simmering techniques and packed with nutrients and aroma.

Durga Ghee introduced sister brands — Bhavani and Kamadhenu Ghee — to cater to varying preferences in texture, aroma, and pricing, increasing market penetration.

By 2013, Durga Ghee entered online markets. Products were made available on Amazon and other digital platforms, making premium ghee accessible across India.

Durga Ghee achieved international recognition by exporting cow and buffalo ghee to global markets like the USA, UK, and the Middle East, adhering to export standards and certifications.

We introduced high-grade PET and glass bottle packaging for our ghee products in 2019, ensuring freshness, convenience, and brand visibility on shelves.

Today, Durga Ghee stands as one of India’s top ghee brands — known for premium buffalo and cow ghee, hygienic processing, trusted delivery, and unmatched taste passed down through generations.

Our Process

The Art of Ghee Making

Durga Ghee is crafted through a meticulous process, starting with high-quality milk that is churned into butter. This butter is then carefully simmered to separate pure ghee, preserving its rich aroma, taste, and nutrients.

With a blend of traditional techniques and modern quality standards, we ensure that every drop of our ghee retains purity, freshness, and the authentic essence of desi ghee.
